# Stand
Data is a substance with a grain, not a number to be smoothed. Show the
texture — the jitter, the density, the stutter of a real signal — rather
than averaging it into a clean line. The material's roughness is
information; sanding it off discards the truth the visual was meant to
carry. Let the data look like what it is.

# Counterforce
Raw grain can obscure. A signal shown at full fidelity may be unreadable
noise; the viewer needs composition — aggregation, smoothing, a chosen
frame — to see the shape at all. Clarity is a real obligation, and total
fidelity sometimes serves only the maker's honesty, not the viewer's
understanding.

# When to break
Break when the grain hides the shape and the shape is the point — smooth,
bin, or aggregate to make the trend legible. Break when fidelity is
performative rather than informative. The default is grain; the exception
is a smoothing that demonstrably reveals more than it hides.
