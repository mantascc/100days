# Stand
Polish is subtraction. The finished state is reached by removing what does
not carry weight — not by layering effects until it looks done. Each
element earns its place or leaves. No gradients, no shadows, no decorative
borders: density and geometry create form, and the last edit is usually a
deletion.

# Counterforce
A thing can be stripped past meaning. Expressiveness — bloom, depth, a
material glow — is not always ornament; sometimes it is the content. A lens
that does not refract, glass that does not distort, conveys nothing.
Richness is a legitimate medium when the subject *is* light, depth, or
optics, and reduction would erase the very thing being shown.

# When to break
Break when the effect *is* the material, not a coat of paint on it — when
the subject is glass, lensing, glow, or refraction and removing the
richness removes the point. Break for a deliberate, isolated indulgence
that the rest of the quiet earns. Never break to make something "feel
finished"; that instinct is the one reduction exists to resist.
