# What broke
The core rule "pick ONE accent per project" (a quiet-over-loud corollary)
is broken by design here: Ube Kaizen runs three named colors at once —
violet, lavender, terra — as a harmonized triad, plus pulsing accent demos.
More accent presence than the single-spectral rule allows.

# Why it worked
Mostly. The sketch *is* a color tool, so showing colors in relation is its
job; a single accent would defeat the premise. The triad stays quiet
because the three are desaturated and harmonized rather than competing
spectral singles — loudness comes from saturation and conflict, which this
avoids. Where it strains: the pulsing demos edge toward the theatrics the
core resists.

# Pressure
Suggests quiet-over-loud is about *perceived* loudness (saturation,
conflict, motion), not literal accent count. A muted multi-accent can be
quieter than one neon single. If color-tool sketches recur, the
single-accent rule should be reframed as a saturation/harmony budget rather
than a hard count.
