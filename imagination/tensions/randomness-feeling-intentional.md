# What
How do you make randomness *read* as choice? Raw uniform noise feels like noise; the maker's note that "a linear set is a gradient, not an exploration" gets at it — a flat mapping produces sameness, not surprise. The working answer so far is non-linear, piecewise mapping that carves the random space into distinct phases, so a run feels authored rather than rolled. But "authored randomness" stays a paradox worth keeping open: lean too far and you've just hidden a decision behind a dice.

# Charge
One of the strongest live questions in the practice — it sits under every stochastic sketch and never quite resolves. Keep pressing it; do not close it.

# Prompts
- A piece where the *mapping curve* is the visible artwork, randomness the input.
- Phase-banded randomness: a system that snaps between authored regimes.
- Let the viewer feel the seed — same noise, different mappings, side by side.
