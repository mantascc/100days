# What
Do you expose the workings or finish the surface? An early maxim — "show the mechanism, don't hide it; polish is reduction" — pulls against the later sketches' bloom, glass, and gradients. The two impulses aren't enemies exactly, but they want opposite things: the legible, honest, slightly raw piece that teaches its own logic, versus the resolved, seductive object that hides its seams. Recent work has drifted toward polish; the mechanism-first instinct is the quieter conscience.

# Charge
A persistent values question, currently leaning one way — which is precisely why it's worth keeping in view. The tension keeps the polish honest.

# Prompts
- A piece deliberately split: polished on one half, exposed on the other.
- An artwork whose "view source" is part of the experience.
- Take a glassy, finished sketch and strip it back to its raw mechanism.
