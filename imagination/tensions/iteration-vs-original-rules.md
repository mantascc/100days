# What
When is a sketch genuinely new and when is it a "v2" — refinement dressed as creation? The maker flags it directly: several pieces "may represent iteration" rather than fresh ground, and the custom cellular automaton honestly "lacks the rich library that makes Conway's interesting." There's a real pull between polishing a known system and inventing a strange one. Iteration compounds craft; original rules risk failure but are where the surprises live. The practice needs both and keeps having to choose.

# Charge
A live, slightly uncomfortable question — it's the self-honesty check on a daily practice. Keep it open; it guards against coasting.

# Prompts
- A sketch that is *explicitly* a v3 — own the iteration, make refinement the subject.
- A rule invented from scratch with no reference system, however ugly.
- Diff two versions of the same idea on screen and let the delta be the piece.
