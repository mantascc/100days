# What
Do you explore a space *sequentially* — one variation after another, hunting — or *simultaneously*, laying every option out at once? The maker's crisp framing: "sequential iteration produces search; simultaneous display produces taxonomy." They're different cognitive acts. Search finds the one good point and discards the rest; mapping refuses to choose and shows the whole field. The reference-sheet vein lives entirely on the mapping side, but the generative work has always been search. Which mode a sketch wants is a real, recurring decision.

# Charge
A genuinely useful lens that keeps clarifying the recent work — alive precisely because both modes keep proving necessary.

# Prompts
- A piece that *animates* between search and map: one point expanding into the field.
- A taxonomy you can collapse back into a single wandering search path.
- Let the viewer toggle: explore one variation, then see it placed in the whole space.
