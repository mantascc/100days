# What
Hand-rolled physics as a motion engine: springs, gravity, collisions, orbits, metaball scalar fields. The maker prefers writing the integrator over importing one — there's evident pleasure in feeling the forces directly, tuning a spring constant by hand, watching a three-body system go chaotic. Physics here is rarely realistic; it's expressive, a way to give agents and grids a believable, weighty motion that pure noise can't.

Steady and dependable — a tool more than an obsession, but it gives the work its sense of weight. Quietly underused relative to noise as a motion source.

# Prompts
- A physics where one constant slowly changes and the world reorganises around it.
- Collisions that *leave marks* — simulation as a slowly accreting drawing.
- Couple physics to audio so the bass literally adds mass.
