# What
Typography as the subject of the work, not its caption. Letters become masks, fields, particle targets, and shader materials; type is set in motion, eroded by wind, morphed from a swarm, or distorted under a lens. The maker's interaction-design background shows here — there's real fluency with how letterforms carry meaning even as they're abused — but the vein is still mostly *applying* effects to text rather than letting text generate the system.

# Charge
Charged and a little unfinished — typography keeps getting decorated but rarely drives. The pull to make letters *behave like a system* is unspent.

# Prompts
- A typeface where the rules of an emergence system *are* the letterforms.
- Text that reads differently depending on how fast you scroll past it.
- A paragraph that self-sets — line breaks and weights emerge, never specified.
