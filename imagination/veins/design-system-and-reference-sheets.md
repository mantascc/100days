# What
A pronounced second-half turn: from one-off generative pieces toward reusable artifacts — palettes, progress indicators, thinking-state libraries, layout playgrounds, named systems like "style-seed" and "Clear Channel." Here the interaction designer steps fully forward. These sketches are less about emergence than about *codifying* — building the toolkit and vocabulary a practice runs on. The reference sheet is the genre: an exhaustive, browsable map of a design space rather than a single point in it.

# Charge
The dominant energy of the recent practice — productive and clearly where the maker's day-job instincts live. Watch that it doesn't crowd out the wilder generative veins.

# Prompts
- A reference sheet that *generates itself* from a single rule, not hand-authored.
- A design system whose tokens drift over time — a living, aging palette.
- Cross the reference-sheet impulse with emergence: a taxonomy that self-organises.
