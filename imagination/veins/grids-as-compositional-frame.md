# What
The regular grid as a silent reference frame against which everything else is measured. Cells sample a field, hold a glyph, frame a variation, downsample a camera feed, or quantise a metaball. The grid is rarely the subject — it's the stage — but it's so pervasive that it shapes the whole visual language of the practice: discrete, sampled, legible. The interesting tension is between the grid's rigidity and the continuous phenomena poured into it.

# Charge
Steady and structural — almost a precondition rather than an obsession. Worth deliberately abandoning to see what the work looks like off the lattice.

# Prompts
- A grid that breathes: cells that resize, merge, or drift from their slots.
- Non-square tilings — hex, Voronoi, aperiodic — as the sampling frame.
- The grid as the figure, the content as the ground.
