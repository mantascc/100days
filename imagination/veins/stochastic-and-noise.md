# What
Randomness treated as a first-class material rather than a fallback. Random walks, Markov and Brownian motion, Perlin and fBm fields, per-session seeding — the maker reaches for noise not to fill gaps but to make each run a distinct living thing. "Make every session unique" recurs almost as a creed. The interesting work isn't in the randomness itself but in *shaping* it: where the distribution bends, where the noise is structured into texture.

# Charge
Deeply alive and entangled with the intentionality tension — the question of when randomness *reads* as choice is one of the practice's best open problems.

# Prompts
- Noise you can hear the grain of: expose the distribution as the artwork.
- Two noise sources fighting — Brownian roughness against Perlin smoothness.
- A piece where the seed is chosen by the viewer and becomes their signature.
