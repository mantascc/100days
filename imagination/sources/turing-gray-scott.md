# What
Alan Turing's 1952 insight that two diffusing, reacting chemicals can break symmetry and form stable spatial patterns — spots, stripes, the morphology of animal coats. The Gray-Scott system is the most-used computational instance: two coupled PDEs whose feed and kill rates, tuned slightly, span an astonishing variety of patterns from mitosis-like blobs to coral mazes. It's emergence in a *continuous* medium, the chemistry counterpart to discrete cellular automata, and a reminder that pattern can be a physical inevitability.
