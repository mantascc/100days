# What
The Kuramoto model of coupled oscillators: a population of phase oscillators, each nudged toward its neighbours, spontaneously synchronising once coupling crosses a threshold. The vivid natural instance is fireflies flashing in unison. As a source it extends emergence from *spatial* order (flocking, segregation) to *temporal* order — rhythm and phase locking arising with no conductor. It opens a door the practice has barely walked through: emergence in time rather than space.
