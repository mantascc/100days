# What
The two canonical models of self-organised collective motion. Vicsek (1995) reduces flocking to a single rule — each particle aligns its heading with its neighbours' average, plus noise — and shows a phase transition into coherent motion as noise drops. Reynolds' boids (1987) add separation and cohesion to alignment, the three steering behaviours that still underpin most flocking code. Together they're the cleanest demonstration of the practice's central thesis: order as an emergent phase, not a designed arrangement.
