# What
Thomas Schelling's 1971 model of segregation: agents on a checkerboard relocate if too few neighbours match them, with even a mild same-kind preference producing stark, total segregation. The classic demonstration with coins on a grid. Its power as a source is the counterintuitive gap between micro-motive and macro-outcome — no agent *wants* full segregation, yet the system delivers it. It's emergence with a moral edge, and a clean argument that aggregate patterns can betray individual intent.
