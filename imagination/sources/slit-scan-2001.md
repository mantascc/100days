# What
The slit-scan photographic technique made famous by the star-gate sequence in *2001: A Space Odyssey* — exposing the film through a moving slit so time and space smear into streaks of light. Computationally it becomes "read one column per frame and stack them," turning a temporal signal into a spatial image. As a source it's narrow but vivid: a specific, cinematic way of rendering motion as accumulated trace rather than instantaneous position.
