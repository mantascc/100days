# What
A visual and intellectual lineage rather than a single work: the Bauhaus's reduction of design to system and primitive, the cybernetic movement's view of art as feedback and process, and the stark vector aesthetic of early computer displays. Together they license the practice's design-system turn — the conviction that a generative artwork and a design language are the same kind of object, both built from rules, tokens, and constraints. It's where the maker's interaction-design instincts find their historical roots.
